Centerville School is one of the finest Class C schools in the state of Montana. Located in a quiet rural setting 13 miles southwest of Great Falls, Centerville School serves the small towns of Sand Coulee, Tracy, Centerville, Stockett and surrounding areas. Our school enrollment is 227 students in grades K-12. Although the school may be small, the opportunities provided to the students in the areas of curriculum, technology, after-school programs and extra-curricular activities are numerous and varied. Our small class sizes, and a student/teacher ratio of 15 to 1, provide our students with the extra help and care that only a small school can provide. We are proud of our students, staff and community and take great pride in the excellent academic, athletic and activity programs we provide. We may be a rural school but we have a big city mentality towards education!

Community and school spirit adds to the deep sense of pride that students develop through attendance at our small school with big opportunities. Centerville’s identification with its mining heritage remains; a miner is the mascot, an ore wagon is displayed in front of the school, and photographs of several generations of ethnically diverse graduates (many from these early mining families) are displayed on the walls of the school.

In 2011, our School District implemented a 4-day school week with the purpose of increasing instructional time per class as well as improving student grades and attendance. The change has proved to be successful. Centerville teachers and staff are seeing an improvement in academic performance, attendance and attitude. There is increased student involvement in class as well as more time to complete work. The longer days are allowing teachers extra time to work with the students one-on-one. Attendance and test scores are up while behavioral problems are down. Centerville students receive 1,156 classroom hours per year which is 76 more hours than state minimum. The school classes run from 8 a.m. to 4 p.m. Monday through Thursday.
